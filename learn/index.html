Common Questions Pilots Ask

What is specific excess power (Ps)?

Ps is the rate at which your aircraft can gain or lose energy. Positive Ps means you can climb or accelerate. Zero Ps is the edge of your envelope.

What's the corner velocity?

Corner velocity is the speed where you can pull maximum G at the stall boundary. It's where your aircraft turns tightest. Related to Va but not identical.

Why does Vy change with altitude?

Best rate of climb speed decreases with altitude because of how power available and power required curves shift. The EM diagram shows this clearly.

Can I teach maneuvers with the overlay tool?

Yes. The overlay tool shows students exactly what happens to energy throughout a maneuver. It's ground instruction that actually transfers to the aircraft.

Is Vmc really a single number?

The published Vmc is tested under ideal conditions. Real Vmc varies with weight, altitude, and bank angle. AeroEdge calculates the dynamic value.

How much altitude do I really lose in a steep turn?

It depends on your aircraft, entry speed, and power setting. The overlay tool shows exact altitude loss based on actual physics.
